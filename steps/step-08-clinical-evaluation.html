Clinical evaluation & performance

Clinical evaluation is the systematic, ongoing process of generating and assessing clinical data to confirm safety, performance, and an acceptable benefit-risk. For software it has a specific structure. File: docs/regulatory/technical_documentation/clinical_evaluation.md.

MDR clinical evaluation basics

Under MDR Art. 61 and Annex XIV, the manufacturer plans and conducts a clinical evaluation and writes a Clinical Evaluation Report (CER), kept up to date with post-market data. The relevant guidance is the MEDDEV 2.7/1 Rev. 4 heritage plus MDCG documents.

The three pillars of SaMD clinical evidence

For software, the IMDRF "Clinical Evaluation of SaMD" framework defines three things you must show:

PillarQuestionDermaScan
Valid clinical associationIs there a sound scientific basis linking the output to the clinical condition?Dermoscopic features genuinely relate to malignancy (established dermatology)
Analytical / technical validationDoes the software correctly and reliably process input into output?Model computes the score correctly, reproducibly (verification)
Clinical validationDoes the output achieve the intended clinical purpose in the target population?Sensitivity/specificity on an independent, representative test set; ideally external validation

Watch out - never report one number
"95% accuracy" is meaningless and a red flag. On imbalanced data a model can score high accuracy while missing most melanomas. Always report sensitivity AND specificity (or AUROC) with confidence intervals, the dataset/split, the operating threshold, and subgroup breakdowns.
Tip - sensitivity vs specificity trade-off
For a cancer-screening triage tool you tune the threshold for high sensitivity (catch the cancers), accepting lower specificity (more false alarms a clinician then rules out). Be ready to justify your chosen operating point in clinical terms, not just "best F1".

Independent test set & external validation

  • Strictly separate train / validation / test; never tune on the test set.
  • Prevent leakage (e.g. multiple images of the same lesion split across train and test).
  • Prefer external validation on data from a different source/site than training - generalisation is the real-world question.
Hot 2026 - clinical evidence for AI
Notified Bodies increasingly expect prospective or at least independent external clinical validation for AI diagnostics, plus evidence the model is robust to real-world distribution shift and to image-quality variation. "It scored well on a held-out split of the same dataset" is no longer enough. The human-AI team performance (does the clinician + tool beat the clinician alone, without inducing automation bias?) is a growing evaluation question.

Benefit-risk determination

The clinical evaluation closes the loop with ISO 14971: the demonstrated clinical benefit (earlier/better triage) must outweigh the residual risks (false negatives, bias, over-reliance). This benefit-risk conclusion is required by GSPR 1 and feeds the risk management report.

Interview Q&A

What are the three components of clinical evaluation for SaMD?

Valid clinical association (scientific link between output and condition), analytical/technical validation (the software computes the output correctly and reliably), and clinical validation (the output achieves the intended clinical purpose in the target population).

Why not just report accuracy?

Accuracy hides class imbalance: a model can miss most melanomas yet look accurate because benign cases dominate. You report sensitivity and specificity (or AUROC) with confidence intervals, the threshold, the dataset/split, and subgroup results.

For a melanoma triage tool, do you prioritise sensitivity or specificity?

Sensitivity, because a false negative (missed melanoma) is the most serious harm. You set the threshold for high sensitivity and accept more false positives, which a clinician then rules out, and you justify the operating point clinically.

What is external validation and why does it matter?

Evaluating the model on data from a different site/source than training. It tests real-world generalisation and exposes distribution shift, which an internal held-out split can mask. Notified Bodies increasingly expect it for AI diagnostics.

How does clinical evaluation connect to risk management?

It provides the clinical benefit side of the benefit-risk determination required by the GSPR and ISO 14971: the demonstrated benefit must outweigh the residual risks for the device to be acceptable, and it is kept current with post-market clinical follow-up.
