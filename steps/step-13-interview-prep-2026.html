Interview prep & 2026 hot topics

Your edge is being the rare candidate who can speak regulation and AI engineering in the same breath. This page is the night-before revision sheet.

How to present DermaScan (the 90-second pitch)

Tip - the pitch (honest, structured)

"DermaScan is a portfolio project where I built an AI classifier for skin-lesion malignancy and, as a learning exercise, produced the full regulatory file it would need as an EU Class IIa AI Software as a Medical Device. I defined the intended purpose, justified the Rule 11 classification, built an ISO 14971 risk file with AI-specific hazards like dataset shift and Fitzpatrick bias, mapped the EU AI Act high-risk obligations onto an integrated technical file, and set up a traceability matrix from requirements through risks to tests. It is explicitly a simulation, not a certified device, but it let me practise exactly the regulatory-plus-AI workflow that MDR and the AI Act now demand."

Use STAR for the deep-dive version

  • Situation - wanted to bridge my QA/test-engineering background into MedTech regulatory affairs for AI devices.
  • Task - build a realistic end-to-end SaMD regulatory file around a working ML model.
  • Action - intended purpose -> classification -> risk -> IEC 62304 lifecycle -> AI Act mapping -> data governance/bias analysis -> V&V with traceability -> PMS plan.
  • Result - a coherent, auditable documentation set and fluency in how MDR and the AI Act interlock; I can now read a real technical file and know what is missing.
Watch out - integrity
Always say it is a simulation/training project. Never imply it is CE marked or clinically validated on patients. Interviewers respect the honesty and it lets you talk about why the rules exist, which is the real signal of competence.

Rapid-fire Q&A (drill these out loud)

Q1. What is the difference between MDR and the AI Act for your device?

MDR regulates it as a medical device (safety/performance, classification, clinical evidence); the AI Act regulates the AI itself as a high-risk system (data governance, transparency, human oversight, robustness). They overlap and are meant to be satisfied through one integrated file and one assessment.

Q2. Walk me through the risk management process.

Plan -> analysis (hazards, foreseeable misuse, risk estimation) -> evaluation against acceptability -> control in priority order -> residual and overall residual risk -> benefit-risk -> RM report -> feed production/post-production data back. (ISO 14971.)

Q3. Hazard vs harm?

Hazard is a potential source of harm; harm is the actual injury; the hazardous situation is exposure to the hazard linking them.

Q4. What is Rule 11?

The MDR software classification rule: decision-support software is Class IIa, IIb if a wrong decision could cause serious deterioration/surgery, III if death/irreversible harm.

Q5. IEC 62304 safety classes?

A (no injury possible), B (non-serious injury), C (death/serious injury), assigned from the hazard analysis and reducible via external controls.

Q6. What is SOUP?

Software of Unknown Provenance - third-party/OSS components; you list them, specify requirements, and assess known anomalies/CVEs.

Q7. Verification vs validation?

Verification = built to spec (against requirements); validation = built the right thing (against user need / intended use).

Q8. What are the GSPR?

MDR Annex I General Safety and Performance Requirements - the essential requirements, evidenced via a GSPR checklist and harmonized standards.

Q9. Name the core AI Act high-risk articles.

9 risk mgmt, 10 data governance, 11 technical doc, 12 logging, 13 transparency, 14 human oversight, 15 accuracy/robustness/security.

Q10. Why is your device high-risk under the AI Act?

Article 6(1): it is (a safety component of) an MDR product requiring third-party conformity assessment.

Q11. Biggest data risk in AI dermatology?

Under-representation of dark skin tones (Fitzpatrick IV-VI), risking lower sensitivity for those groups; mitigate by measuring, documenting, restricting intended use, and monitoring.

Q12. Why not report accuracy alone?

Class imbalance hides missed cancers; report sensitivity, specificity/AUROC with CIs, threshold, dataset/split, and subgroups.

Q13. Sensitivity or specificity for cancer triage?

Prioritise sensitivity; a missed melanoma is the worst harm; accept more false positives and justify the threshold clinically.

Q14. Three pillars of SaMD clinical evidence?

Valid clinical association, analytical/technical validation, clinical validation.

Q15. What is a traceability matrix?

A bidirectional map from needs/risks to requirements to design to tests, proving full coverage and no orphans.

Q16. PMS vs PMCF?

PMS is the whole post-market data system; PMCF is its proactive clinical-data-collection sub-part.

Q17. Serious-incident reporting timeline?

Without delay, at most 15 days; 10 days for serious public-health threat; 2 days for death/serious deterioration.

Q18. What is EUDAMED and its 2026 status?

The EU device database; mandatory use phases in around 28 May 2026.

Q19. What is UDI?

Unique Device Identification - a device identifier plus production identifier enabling traceability.

Q20. What does CE marking signify, and the DoC?

CE = the visible symbol of conformity; the Declaration of Conformity is the manufacturer's legal statement behind it.

Q21. Notified Body vs competent authority?

NB does conformity assessment and issues certificates; the competent authority (BfArM in DE) oversees the market and the NBs.

Q22. What is a PRRC?

Person Responsible for Regulatory Compliance, MDR Art. 15 - a qualified role ensuring conformity and reporting.

Q23. ISO 13485 vs ISO/IEC 42001?

13485 is the device QMS; 42001 adds an AI management system (data, model lifecycle, bias, oversight).

Q24. What is a PCCP?

A predetermined change control plan that pre-authorises defined model updates/retraining without a fresh conformity assessment each time.

Q25. How do you handle AI model drift after release?

Monitor live and subgroup performance in PMS with thresholds that trigger CAPA or controlled retraining under a PCCP.

Q26. Cybersecurity hooks in MDR?

GSPR 17 (state-of-the-art incl. information security), guided by MDCG 2019-16, implemented per IEC 81001-5-1; harmful vulnerabilities enter the ISO 14971 file.

Q27. What is MDCG?

The Medical Device Coordination Group - issues the practical EU guidance documents (e.g. 2019-11 software qualification, 2019-16 cybersecurity).

Q28. How does GDPR affect your device?

Health data is special-category (Art. 9); needs lawful basis, minimisation, security (Art. 32), and usually a DPIA.

Q29. What is human oversight (AI Act Art. 14) in practice?

Design so a competent human can understand, monitor, and override the AI and is not lulled into over-reliance - e.g. mandatory clinician confirmation and clear confidence display.

Q30. Why is Rule 11 / MDR being revised?

It over-classifies clinical software and the NB system is overloaded; the Digital Omnibus and MDR revision aim to simplify and reduce duplication with the AI Act.

Q31. What is DiGA?

Germany's fast-track for prescribable digital health applications via BfArM, with its own evidence and reimbursement pathway.

Q32. How do you avoid double work between MDR and the AI Act?

Build one integrated technical file and use one conformity assessment (Art. 8(2)), reusing the risk system, clinical/performance data, and QMS.

Q33. What is the benefit-risk determination?

The judgement that the device's clinical benefit outweighs its residual risks; required by GSPR 1 and concluded in the risk management report and clinical evaluation.

Q34. Where do you start with a new product?

The intended purpose - it drives qualification, classification, applicable GSPRs, risk scope, clinical evidence, and post-market plans.

Smart questions to ask the interviewer

  • How are you integrating the AI Act with your existing MDR technical files - one file or two?
  • How are you approaching EUDAMED registration ahead of the 2026 deadlines?
  • Are you using ISO/IEC 42001 or predetermined change control plans for your AI products?
  • How does the RA team work with data science on bias and post-market drift monitoring?
