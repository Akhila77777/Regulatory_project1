Cybersecurity

Security is now an explicit safety requirement: an insecure device is an unsafe device. German regulators are increasingly scrutinising this for connected and AI products. File: a cybersecurity section in the technical documentation.

The legal hooks

InstrumentCybersecurity requirement
MDR Annex I, GSPR 17.2 & 17.4Software developed per state of the art including information security; IT security measures incl. protection against unauthorised access.
MDCG 2019-16The key guidance on medical device cybersecurity (secure design, risk, IFU security info, post-market).
IEC 81001-5-1Harmonized standard for the secure development lifecycle of health software (the "security IEC 62304").
EU AI Act Art. 15High-risk AI must be resilient to errors and to attempts to alter use/performance via exploitation (incl. data poisoning, model poisoning, adversarial examples, model evasion).
GDPRArt. 32 security of processing; breach notification.
Cyber Resilience Act (CRA)Horizontal EU law on security of products with digital elements; interplay with MDR is being clarified.
NIS2 (DE: implementation)Security duties for essential/important entities incl. healthcare operators.

Core concepts to know

  • Security risk management - run a security risk assessment alongside ISO 14971 (threat modelling, e.g. STRIDE); security risks that can cause patient harm feed the safety risk file.
  • Secure-by-design & defence in depth - authentication, authorisation, encryption in transit/at rest, input validation, least privilege.
  • SBOM (Software Bill of Materials) - inventory of all components (your SOUP list doubles as this) to track vulnerabilities/CVEs.
  • Vulnerability management - monitor, triage (CVSS), patch; coordinated disclosure.
  • Logging & audit - tamper-evident logs (also satisfies AI Act Art. 12).
Reg - security IS safety
Under MDR, a cybersecurity vulnerability that can lead to patient harm is a safety risk and must be in the ISO 14971 risk file with controls and post-market monitoring. Security is not a separate silo; it is part of the benefit-risk story.

Hot 2026 - the convergence headache
Manufacturers must now reconcile MDR + AI Act Art. 15 + the Cyber Resilience Act + NIS2 + GDPR for one connected device. German authorities and the BSI are scrutinising this closely. Being able to say "I understand IEC 81001-5-1 as the implementation standard and MDCG 2019-16 as the guidance, and how AI Act Art. 15 adds adversarial robustness" is a strong, current differentiator.

Interview Q&A

How does cybersecurity fit into MDR?

Through GSPR 17 in Annex I: software must be developed to the state of the art including information security, with IT-security measures against unauthorised access. Cybersecurity risks that can cause harm go into the ISO 14971 risk file, guided by MDCG 2019-16 and implemented per IEC 81001-5-1.

What is an SBOM and why does it matter?

A Software Bill of Materials: a complete inventory of components and dependencies. It lets you track which products are affected when a new vulnerability (CVE) is disclosed and is increasingly required by regulators. For DermaScan the SOUP list is the SBOM.

What cybersecurity threats are specific to AI?

Adversarial examples, data poisoning, model poisoning, model inversion/theft, and evasion attacks. AI Act Article 15 explicitly requires resilience against these for high-risk systems, in addition to conventional security.

How do safety and security risk management relate?

They run in parallel and intersect: a security threat that can result in patient harm is also a safety risk, so it is captured in the ISO 14971 file with controls and monitored post-market. Security is part of the overall benefit-risk determination.
