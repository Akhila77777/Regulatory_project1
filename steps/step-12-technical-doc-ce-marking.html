Technical documentation, conformity assessment & CE marking

This is the assembly point: the technical file is the evidence, conformity assessment is the check, CE marking is the result. Folder: docs/regulatory/technical_documentation/.

The technical documentation (MDR Annex II & III)

Reg - what the technical file contains
Annex II (technical documentation): device description and specification; intended purpose; information for labelling/IFU; design and manufacturing information; the GSPR checklist; benefit-risk analysis and risk management; product verification and validation (incl. clinical evaluation, software V&V). Annex III: the post-market surveillance documentation (PMS plan, PSUR/PMS report, PMCF). Together they are "the technical file".

Everything you built in sections 01-11 slots into this structure - the technical file is essentially an index over all of it.

Conformity assessment routes

For Class IIa (DermaScan's assumption), the manufacturer needs a Notified Body. The common MDR route:

  • Annex IX - QMS audit + assessment of technical documentation (on a sampling basis for IIa). This is the typical route.
  • Annex XI - product conformity verification (alternative).

For an AI device, the same Notified Body assessment can, under the AI Act's integration provisions, also cover the high-risk AI requirements - one assessment, both regimes (see section 06).

The market-access chain (memorise the sequence)

  1. Define intended purpose -> classify (Rule 11).
  2. Build the QMS (ISO 13485) and technical documentation (Annex II/III).
  3. Demonstrate GSPR conformity via harmonized standards + risk + clinical evaluation + V&V.
  4. Engage a Notified Body for conformity assessment (Class IIa+).
  5. NB issues the CE certificate.
  6. Manufacturer draws up the EU Declaration of Conformity (DoC).
  7. Affix the CE mark (with the NB's 4-digit number for IIa+).
  8. Register the device and actors in EUDAMED; assign UDI.
  9. Place on market -> run PMS / vigilance for the lifetime.
Tip - CE mark vs Declaration of Conformity
The Declaration of Conformity is the manufacturer's legal statement that the device meets all applicable requirements; the CE mark is the visible symbol of that. The manufacturer (not the Notified Body) always owns the DoC. For self-certified Class I there is no NB number next to the CE mark; for IIa+ there is.

Hot 2026 - capacity & simplification
Two live themes: Notified Body capacity (a bottleneck under MDR, driving long queues and transition-deadline extensions), and the Digital Omnibus / MDR revision aiming to cut duplicate documentation across MDR, IVDR and the AI Act and to ease the burden on SMEs. Mentioning the NB-capacity crunch shows real market awareness.

Interview Q&A

What is in the MDR technical documentation?

Per Annex II: device description and intended purpose, labelling/IFU, design and manufacturing info, the GSPR checklist, benefit-risk and risk management, and verification/validation including clinical evaluation and software V&V; per Annex III, the post-market surveillance documentation. It is the complete evidence of conformity.

Walk me through getting a Class IIa device to market.

Define intended purpose and classify, build the QMS and technical file, show GSPR conformity through standards, risk, clinical evaluation and V&V, undergo Notified Body conformity assessment (typically Annex IX), obtain the CE certificate, issue the Declaration of Conformity, affix the CE mark with the NB number, register in EUDAMED with a UDI, and run PMS/vigilance afterwards.

Difference between the CE mark and the Declaration of Conformity?

The Declaration of Conformity is the manufacturer's signed legal statement of compliance; the CE mark is the visible symbol that such a declaration exists. The manufacturer owns the DoC; for Class IIa+ the CE mark carries the Notified Body's number.

What is a PRRC?

The Person Responsible for Regulatory Compliance (MDR Art. 15): a designated, suitably qualified person who ensures conformity, technical documentation, PMS and reporting obligations are met. It is a defined RA role and a realistic career goal.

Why are Notified Bodies a bottleneck right now?

MDR vastly increased the assessment workload and the number of devices needing NB involvement, while the number of designated NBs and their capacity grew more slowly, causing backlogs and prompting transition-deadline extensions and simplification proposals.
