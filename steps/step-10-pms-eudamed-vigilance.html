Post-market surveillance, EUDAMED & vigilance

MDR put major weight on what happens after the device is on the market. For AI, where performance can drift, this is doubly important. File: a PMS plan in the technical documentation.

Post-Market Surveillance (PMS)

Reg - PMS (MDR Art. 83-86)
Every manufacturer must have a proactive PMS system integrated into the QMS, based on a PMS plan (Art. 84). Outputs:
  • PMS Report - for Class I devices.
  • PSUR (Periodic Safety Update Report) - for Class IIa and above; summarises PMS data, benefit-risk, and conclusions. DermaScan (IIa) would produce a PSUR.
PMS feeds back into the risk file, the clinical evaluation, and CAPA.

Post-Market Clinical Follow-up (PMCF)

PMCF (Annex XIV Part B) is the proactive collection of clinical data in real use to confirm safety/performance and detect emerging risks. For DermaScan this means monitoring real-world sensitivity/specificity, especially across subgroups, to catch model drift.

Vigilance & incident reporting

ConceptMeaning
Serious incidentAn event that led, or could lead, to death or serious deterioration of health.
Reporting timelines (Art. 87)Generally without delay and no later than 15 days; 10 days for serious public-health threat; 2 days for death/serious deterioration.
FSCA / FSNField Safety Corrective Action and the Field Safety Notice that informs users (e.g. a recall, update, or usage restriction).
Trend reportingStatistically significant increases in non-serious incidents or expected side-effects must be reported.

EUDAMED & UDI

Reg - EUDAMED
EUDAMED is the EU database for medical devices, with modules for actor registration, UDI/device registration, certificates, clinical investigations, vigilance, and market surveillance. UDI (Unique Device Identification) is the standardised code (Device Identifier + Production Identifier) that makes each device traceable. For software the UDI sits in documentation and an "about" screen, and changes with significant software versions.
Hot 2026 - EUDAMED becomes mandatory
EUDAMED has been voluntary while modules were finalised. Its mandatory use is phasing in around 28 May 2026 (actor, UDI/device, and notified-body/certificate modules first, with vigilance and market-surveillance following). This is a top-of-mind operational topic - manufacturers are scrambling to register. Saying "I know EUDAMED is becoming mandatory in 2026 and what the modules are" is an instant credibility marker.

AI Act post-market duties (parallel set)

  • Art. 72 - post-market monitoring plan for the AI system (collect and analyse performance data over its lifetime).
  • Art. 73 - report serious incidents to the market surveillance authority.
  • For AI, monitoring must explicitly watch for performance drift and emergent bias - tie this to PMCF.
Tip - the AI drift story
A great closing point in interviews: "For an AI device, post-market surveillance is not paperwork - it is the safety mechanism, because the model can silently degrade as real-world data drifts. My PMS plan defines drift and subgroup-performance monitoring with thresholds that trigger CAPA or retraining under a predetermined change control plan."

Interview Q&A

What is PMS and what does it produce?

A proactive, planned system for gathering and analysing real-world data on a device after market entry, integrated with the QMS. It produces a PMS report (Class I) or a PSUR (Class IIa and above), feeding the risk file, clinical evaluation, and CAPA.

Difference between PMS and PMCF?

PMS is the whole post-market data system (complaints, literature, registries, etc.). PMCF is the clinical sub-part: proactively collecting clinical data from real use to confirm ongoing safety and performance and detect emerging risks.

What is a serious incident and what are the timelines?

An event that led or could lead to death or serious deterioration of health. Reporting is without undue delay and at most 15 days, 10 days for a serious public-health threat, and 2 days for death or serious deterioration.

What is EUDAMED and what is happening with it in 2026?

The EU's central medical device database (registration, UDI, certificates, vigilance, market surveillance). Its mandatory use is phasing in around 28 May 2026, moving from voluntary to required, which is a major current compliance push.

Why is post-market surveillance especially critical for AI devices?

Because model performance can drift as the real-world data distribution shifts, and bias can emerge in new populations. PMS/PMCF for AI must monitor live performance and subgroup metrics with predefined thresholds that trigger corrective action or controlled retraining.
