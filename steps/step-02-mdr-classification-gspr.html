MDR classification & GSPR

Classification sets your conformity route and your workload. For software the key is Rule 11. Project file: docs/regulatory/classification_rationale.md.

The four risk classes

ClassRiskConformity assessmentExamples
ILowSelf-declaration (NB only for sterile/measuring/reusable surgical)Bandages, basic wellness software
IIaMediumNotified Body requiredMost diagnostic-support SaMD
IIbMedium-highNotified Body requiredSoftware driving therapy decisions with serious impact
IIIHighNotified Body + design dossier scrutinyImplants, software for critical/irreversible decisions
Reg - MDR Rule 11 (Annex VIII)
Software intended to provide information used to take decisions for diagnosis or therapeutic purposes is Class IIa, unless those decisions can cause:
  • death or irreversible deterioration -> Class III;
  • serious deterioration or surgical intervention -> Class IIb.
Software for monitoring physiological processes is IIa (IIb if vital parameters where variation could be immediately dangerous). All other software is Class I.

Applying Rule 11 to DermaScan

DermaScan provides information (a malignancy-risk estimate) used to take a diagnostic decision -> Rule 11 applies. The default is Class IIa. The argument over IIb/III is about harm from a wrong decision:

  • A false negative (missed melanoma) could lead to delayed treatment and irreversible harm - which pushes toward IIb/III.
  • The counter-argument: the device is decision support with mandatory clinician review, so the clinician (not the software) makes the final call, keeping it at IIa.

Whichever you choose, document the rationale - the working assumption in this project is IIa pending the written justification. This "argue it, do not assert it" discipline is exactly what a reviewer wants to see.

Hot 2026 - the Rule 11 debate
Rule 11 is widely criticised for up-classifying almost all clinical software, and the MDR/IVDR revision ("MDR 2.0") and the EU Digital Omnibus simplification package are live debates about easing it and reducing duplicate documentation across MDR and the AI Act. Mentioning this shows you follow the policy conversation, not just the static text.

General Safety and Performance Requirements (GSPR)

The GSPR are MDR Annex I - the essential requirements every device must meet. You demonstrate conformity in a GSPR checklist: for each requirement, state applicability, the solution, and the evidence (standard, test, document).

Reg - GSPR 17.2 (software specifics)
Software must be developed and manufactured in accordance with the state of the art, considering development lifecycle, risk management (including information security), verification and validation. This is the hook that pulls in IEC 62304, ISO 14971 and IEC 81001-5-1.

Harmonized standards & presumption of conformity

A harmonized standard is one cited in the EU Official Journal. Conforming to it gives a presumption of conformity to the matching GSPR - this is why standards matter so much. (Note: for the AI Act many harmonized standards are still being written - a current pain point.)

Interview Q&A

Walk me through classifying a diagnostic-support app under MDR.

Confirm it qualifies as a device (Art. 2 / MDCG 2019-11), then apply Rule 11: software giving information for diagnostic/therapeutic decisions is Class IIa by default, IIb if a wrong decision could cause serious deterioration or surgery, III if death or irreversible deterioration. Then justify the class in writing based on the harm from an erroneous output and the role of clinician oversight.

Why is Rule 11 controversial?

It tends to up-classify almost all clinical decision software to IIa or higher, sharply increasing the documentation and Notified Body burden even for low-risk tools. It is a central target of the MDR revision and the Digital Omnibus simplification debate.

What are the GSPR and how do you demonstrate conformity?

The General Safety and Performance Requirements are MDR Annex I - the essential requirements for safety and performance. You demonstrate conformity with a GSPR checklist mapping each applicable requirement to the solution and the objective evidence, preferably via harmonized standards that grant a presumption of conformity.

What does "presumption of conformity" mean?

If you follow a harmonized standard (one listed in the EU Official Journal), you are presumed to meet the corresponding legal requirement, shifting the burden of proof. It is the practical reason RA work revolves around standards.
