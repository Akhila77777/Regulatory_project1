ISO 13485 quality management system

ISO 13485:2016 is the QMS standard for medical devices. MDR effectively requires a QMS (Art. 10), and ISO 13485 is the harmonized way to have one. In DermaScan we borrow its discipline (document control, design controls, CAPA) without claiming a certified QMS. Folder: docs/regulatory/qms/.

What a QMS is for

A QMS is the documented system of processes that ensures the organisation consistently produces safe, effective, compliant devices - and can prove it. It is process-based and risk-based, and it is what a Notified Body audits.

Reg - design controls (the part RA cares about most)
Design inputs (requirements derived from intended use + risk) must be traceable to design outputs (the actual specs/code). Verification answers "did we build the device right?" (outputs meet inputs). Validation answers "did we build the right device?" (it meets user needs and intended use in the real setting). The traceability matrix is the evidence that nothing is orphaned.
Tip - verification vs validation (a guaranteed question)
Verification = meeting the specification (against requirements). Validation = meeting the user's actual need (against intended use). Memorise: "Verification = building it right; Validation = building the right thing."

Design History File / Device Master Record / Device History Record

  • DHF (Design History File) - the record of how the device was designed (plans, reviews, V&V).
  • DMR (Device Master Record) - the "recipe": specs and procedures to make it.
  • DHR (Device History Record) - the record that a specific unit was made to the DMR.

(These terms come from the FDA QSR/21 CFR 820 world; ISO 13485 uses "design file" / "medical device file" but the concepts overlap and interviewers mix them.)

Hot 2026 - ISO/IEC 42001 and the "AI QMS"
For AI products the conversation is extending ISO 13485 with ISO/IEC 42001:2023, the AI Management System standard. It adds governance for data, model lifecycle, bias, transparency, and human oversight. Expect "how would your QMS handle AI?" - the answer is an integrated 13485 + 42001 management system feeding the AI Act obligations.
Hot 2026 - FDA QMSR harmonization
The US FDA's new QMSR (effective 2026) aligns the old 21 CFR 820 with ISO 13485. So 13485 is now the global backbone for both EU and US - a strong thing to mention for any company that exports to the US.

Interview Q&A

What is the purpose of a QMS and why ISO 13485?

A QMS is the documented, risk-based system of processes ensuring devices are consistently safe, effective and compliant, with evidence. ISO 13485 is the medical-device-specific, harmonized QMS standard, so conforming to it supports MDR Article 10 QMS obligations and is what Notified Bodies audit.

Explain verification vs validation.

Verification confirms design outputs meet design inputs (building it right, against the spec). Validation confirms the device meets user needs and intended use in the real-world setting (building the right thing). Both are required and both feed the traceability matrix.

What is CAPA and when is it triggered?

Corrective and Preventive Action: corrective addresses the root cause of a problem that already occurred; preventive addresses a potential problem. It is triggered by nonconformities, audit findings, complaints, or post-market signals. The process is investigate root cause, implement action, and verify effectiveness.

What are design controls?

The structured process governing design: planning, inputs, outputs, design review, verification, validation, transfer, and change control, all captured in a design file with full traceability from user needs through requirements to tests.

How would a QMS adapt for AI-based devices?

By extending ISO 13485 with ISO/IEC 42001 AI-management controls: data governance, model versioning and lineage, bias monitoring, human oversight, and predetermined change control for retraining, all feeding the EU AI Act duties.
