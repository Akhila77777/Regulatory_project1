1. What DermaScan is (TL;DR)

DermaScan is an AI image classifier that screens dermoscopic/clinical skin-lesion images for malignancy (melanoma vs. benign), documented as if it were going through EU conformity assessment as a Software as a Medical Device (SaMD). It is intended as decision-support that assists a qualified clinician in triaging pigmented lesions, never an autonomous diagnosis.

Three regimes stack on it, and the project's whole value is showing they fit together:

  • The device is regulated by the EU MDR (Class IIa software).
  • The AI is regulated by the EU AI Act (high-risk AI system).
  • Both are operationalized by harmonized standards (ISO 14971 risk, IEC 62304 lifecycle, ISO 13485 QMS, IEC 81001-5-1 security, ISO/IEC 42001 AI management).

4. The golden thread

The single most important idea: everything traces, both ways.

Intended use → Risk → Requirement (REQ) → Design → Verification (TST) → Post-market

Why it matters
No requirement without a source; no risk control without a verifying test; no test without a requirement. TRC-001 is the auditable proof of this, and a Notified Body audit lives on it.

5.1 Intended purpose & SaMD

IU-001

Core concepts

  • Qualification: software is a medical device if it acts on data beyond storage/retrieval for a medical purpose for an individual patient (MDCG 2019-11). DermaScan qualifies, so it is SaMD (vs SiMD, software embedded in hardware).
  • Intended purpose is the anchor: it fixes classification, applicable GSPRs, risk scope, clinical evidence, and post-market plans. Change it and everything downstream must be revisited.
  • Conservative claim: "assists a qualified clinician in triaging pigmented lesions", never "detects skin cancer". Overclaiming raises the class and the liability.
Regulation
MDR Art. 2 (definitions), Annex I (intended purpose in the IFU); MDCG 2019-11 (software qualification).

5.2 Classification & GSPR

CLS-001

Core concepts

  • Rule 11: software providing information for diagnostic/therapeutic decisions is Class IIa; IIb if a wrong decision could cause serious deterioration or surgery; III if death/irreversible harm.
  • Decision: Class IIa, argued (not asserted) on the decision-support + mandatory-clinician-review framing. Conditional: autonomous use would push it higher.
  • GSPR: MDR Annex I essential requirements, shown via a checklist mapping each to evidence; a harmonized standard grants a presumption of conformity.
Regulation
MDR Annex VIII Rule 11; Art. 51 (classes); Annex I (GSPR). AI Act Art. 6(1) makes it high-risk.

5.3 Risk management (ISO 14971)

RMF-PLAN-001 RMF-RA-001 RMF-RPT-001

Core concepts

  • The five terms: hazard (source of harm) → sequence of events → hazardous situation (exposure) → harm; risk = severity x probability.
  • Control priority order: (1) inherently safe design, (2) protective measures, (3) information for safety. The IFU is the weakest, used last.
  • AI-specific hazards in the 10-item register: false negative, false positive, out-of-distribution input, Fitzpatrick/algorithmic bias, automation bias, dataset shift, miscalibration, adversarial input, use-error, data leak.
  • Residual & benefit-risk: the missed-melanoma risks stay "Undesirable → Tolerable-AFAP" because severity (S4) is intrinsic; acceptance leans on mandatory clinician review + demonstrated benefit.
  • One integrated file satisfies ISO 14971, MDR, and AI Act Art. 9 together.
Regulation
ISO 14971:2019 (+ ISO/TR 24971); MDR Annex I ch. I; EU AI Act Art. 9.

5.4 IEC 62304 software lifecycle

SDP-001 SSC-001 SOUP-001

Core concepts

  • Safety class A/B/C is derived from the hazard analysis, not assumed. Decision: Class C - worst credible harm is a missed melanoma (serious/fatal), and mandatory clinician review reduces probability but cannot be relied on to prevent it (automation bias; procedural, unverifiable), and 62304 classifies on severity.
  • The two class decisions differ on purpose: MDR class IIa leans on clinician review; IEC 62304 conservatively does not lean on it, giving Class C.
  • SOUP = third-party/OSS components: identify, pin versions, assess known anomalies/CVEs. The SOUP list doubles as the SBOM.
  • ML additions: model + datasets are versioned config items; retraining is a change under a predetermined change control plan (PCCP).
Regulation
IEC 62304:2006+A1:2015 clauses 4.3 (classification), 5-9 (lifecycle & support processes).

5.5 Quality management system (ISO 13485)

SOP-DC-001 SOP-CAPA-001 SOP-DESIGN-001

Core concepts

  • Document control: IDs, versioning, review/approval, change control, records.
  • CAPA: correction (symptom) vs corrective action (root cause) vs preventive action (potential cause); investigate, act, verify effectiveness. Triggered by complaints, audits, and KPI/PMS breaches.
  • Design controls: inputs → outputs → review → verification → validation → transfer → change, all in a design file. Verification = built it right; Validation = built the right thing.
Regulation
ISO 13485:2016 clauses 4.2 (documents), 7.3 (design), 8.5 (CAPA); MDR Art. 10 (QMS). Currency: ISO/IEC 42001 for AI; FDA QMSR aligns with 13485 in 2026.

5.6 EU AI Act

AIA-001

Core concepts

  • High-risk basis: Art. 6(1) - a safety component of an MDR product needing third-party assessment.
  • Obligations by number: 9 risk mgmt, 10 data governance, 11 technical doc (Annex IV), 12 logging, 13 transparency, 14 human oversight, 15 accuracy/robustness/security.
  • Integration: Art. 8(2) allows one technical file and one conformity assessment shared with MDR - no duplication.
  • Provider vs deployer; provider (manufacturer) holds most duties.
Timeline (know it)
High-risk standalone obligations from 2 Aug 2026; AI embedded in MDR/IVDR devices from ~2 Aug 2027; the Digital Omnibus proposes further slippage because harmonized AI standards are not ready.
Regulation
Regulation (EU) 2024/1689, Art. 6, 8-15, 43, 47-49, 72-73, Annex IV.

5.7 Data governance & bias

DG-001 DSC-001

Core concepts

  • Art. 10: data must be relevant, representative, and as error-free/complete as possible, examined for bias, for the intended purpose and setting.
  • Datasets: HAM10000, ISIC, BCN20000, PH2 - public, licensed; documented in the dataset card with demographics.
  • The #1 talking point: public dermoscopy data skews light-skinned (Fitzpatrick I-III); models under-perform on IV-VI. Handle by measuring per-subgroup performance, documenting the gap, restricting the validated range, and monitoring post-market (this is RISK-004).
  • Data lineage & versioning tie each model to its exact data; GDPR Art. 9/32 + DPIA overlay.
Regulation
EU AI Act Art. 10; GDPR Art. 9 (special category), Art. 32 (security); EHDS (context).

5.8 Clinical evaluation

CLIN-001

Core concepts

  • Three pillars (IMDRF / MDCG 2020-1): valid clinical association, technical/analytical validation, clinical validation.
  • Metrics: sensitivity (priority - a missed melanoma is worst), specificity, AUROC, calibration, and per-subgroup, always with confidence intervals. Never a single accuracy figure.
  • Independent / external validation and leakage prevention; the benefit-risk conclusion feeds the risk report.
Regulation
MDR Art. 61 & Annex XIV Part A; MDCG 2020-1 (software clinical evaluation).

5.9 SRS, architecture & traceability

SRS-001 ARC-001 TRC-001

Core concepts

  • Good requirement: unambiguous, testable, atomic, traceable, "shall", with a measurable acceptance criterion and a source (intended use / risk control / AI Act article).
  • Architecture: 10 components with a gate-before-model data flow (validate → quality gate → OOD → model), so an invalid input can never be returned as a confident low-risk score.
  • Traceability matrix proves coverage: every REQ, RISK, and TST is linked; no orphans; AI Act articles mapped.
Regulation
IEC 62304 5.2/5.3; ISO 13485 7.3.6/7.3.7 (V&V); MDR GSPR 17.

5.10 Deployment & MLOps (GCP)

DEP-001

Core concepts

  • Runtime: Cloud Run (API), Artifact Registry, Cloud Storage (model/data), IAM + Secret Manager, Cloud Armor (rate limiting/WAF), Cloud Logging/Audit Logs.
  • MLOps pipeline (Vertex AI): data validation → train → evaluation gate (fails the build on subgroup/fairness breach) → model registry → human approval (PCCP) → deploy → monitor.
  • Monitoring: Vertex AI Model Monitoring for drift/skew (RISK-006, RISK-004) feeds the KPI board and PMS.
  • Governance ties: EU region (GDPR residency), cloud provider as a supplier (ISO 13485 7.4 + DPA), logs satisfy AI Act Art. 12.
Why it is strong
The pipeline's evaluation gate = automated V&V; the approval step = design review / PCCP; the monitoring = post-market surveillance. The MLOps loop and the regulatory lifecycle are the same loop.

5.11 Cybersecurity

CYB-001

Core concepts

  • Security is safety: a vulnerability that can cause harm is a safety risk and lives in the ISO 14971 file (RISK-008, RISK-010).
  • STRIDE threat model over the architecture; secure-by-design (auth, encryption, input validation, least privilege, tamper-evident logs).
  • AI-specific threats: adversarial examples, data/model poisoning, model inversion/extraction, evasion (AI Act Art. 15).
  • SBOM = SOUP list; CVE monitoring → CVSS triage → patch under change control → CAPA.
Regulation
MDR GSPR 17.2/17.4; MDCG 2019-16; IEC 81001-5-1; AI Act Art. 15; GDPR Art. 32; CRA/NIS2 (context).

5.12 Post-market surveillance & KPI board

PMS-001 KPI-001

Core concepts

  • PMS is proactive and continuous; a Class IIa device produces a PSUR (not a plain PMS report). PMCF is the clinical sub-part.
  • Vigilance: serious incident = led/could lead to death or serious deterioration; reporting 15 / 10 / 2 days; FSCA/FSN; trend reporting.
  • EUDAMED mandatory use phasing in ~28 May 2026; UDI for traceability.
  • AI drift is the safety mechanism: the KPI board (Excel data model) tracks performance, subgroup fairness drift, and vigilance/CAPA against thresholds; a breach triggers CAPA or controlled retraining under the PCCP. (Not OEE - a software-service analog: availability / performance / quality.)
Regulation
MDR Art. 83-88, Annex III; AI Act Art. 72 (monitoring), 73 (incidents).

5.13 Technical file & CE route

TF-001

Core concepts

  • Technical documentation = MDR Annex II (device, IFU, design, GSPR, risk, V&V, clinical) + Annex III (post-market). TF-001 indexes every document to its Annex section.
  • Conformity route: Class IIa → Notified Body via Annex IX, integrated with the AI Act (one assessment).
  • Market-access chain: classify → QMS + tech file → GSPR conformity → NB assessment → CE certificate → Declaration of Conformity (manufacturer's) → CE mark + NB number → EUDAMED/UDI → PMS. PRRC (Art. 15) is the responsible person.
Simulation
No NB engaged, no certificate, no DoC, no CE mark, no EUDAMED registration. TF-001 marks each step "not performed".

7. 2026 hot topics

  • EU AI Act x MDR integration - one file, one assessment.
  • AI Act timeline + Digital Omnibus - dates moving; harmonized standards still emerging.
  • EUDAMED mandatory ~28 May 2026.
  • MDR/IVDR "2.0" revision - easing Rule 11 and Notified-Body capacity.
  • ISO/IEC 42001 - AI management system.
  • Bias / Fitzpatrick fairness; PCCP for model change control; cybersecurity convergence (MDR + AI Act + CRA + NIS2 + GDPR).
  • Germany: BfArM, DiGA fast-track, TUV/DEKRA Notified Bodies. The prized profile is Regulatory Affairs + AI dual-fluency - exactly this project.

9. Interview checklist

  • Can I define hazard vs hazardous situation vs harm, with a DermaScan example?
  • Can I justify Rule 11 / Class IIa AND IEC 62304 Class C, and explain why they point differently?
  • Can I list AI Act Art. 9-15 and how they reuse the MDR file?
  • Can I explain verification vs validation in one line each?
  • Can I tell the Fitzpatrick bias story end-to-end (measure → document → restrict → monitor)?
  • Can I trace one requirement from intended use through risk to a test?
  • Can I name the post-market outputs (PSUR) and vigilance timelines, and the EUDAMED 2026 date?
  • Can I give the 90-second honest pitch: an end-to-end Class IIa AI SaMD file, built as a simulation, showing MDR + AI Act fit together?
